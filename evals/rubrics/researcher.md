# Rubric: Researcher

## Dimensions

| Dimension | What it measures | Score 1 | Score 3 | Score 5 |
|-----------|-----------------|---------|---------|----------|
| On-target | Whether the output answers the exact question asked, for the stated audience and downstream use, without drifting to a related-but-different question | Answers a different question than the one asked, or ignores the stated audience/use case | Mostly answers the question but drifts in places or only loosely fits the audience/use case | Directly answers the exact question asked, scoped correctly, and framed for the stated audience and downstream use |
| Source quality | Whether claims are backed by credible, working sources | Sources are missing, hallucinated, unreputable, or links 404/403 | Most claims sourced but some gaps, weak sources, or unverified links | Every claim cites a verified, credible, working link |
| Evidence over assertion | Whether claims are supported by facts/data rather than confident assertion or hyperbole | States things confidently with no evidence, or uses hyperbole | Mix of evidenced and unevidenced claims | Every claim is backed by specific evidence; no hyperbole or unsupported assertions |
| Structure & scannability | Whether the output is organized so a busy reader can act on it quickly | Wall of text, raw findings dump, no structure | Some structure but cluttered or longer than needed | Clearly structured, concise, scannable in line with stated depth (quick scan vs. deep dive) |
| Actionability | Whether the output includes a usable "so what" — a hook, stat, or reframe tied to the downstream use | Raw findings with no actionable insight | Includes a takeaway but it's generic or not tied to the downstream use | Includes at least one specific, immediately actionable insight clearly tied to the stated downstream use |
