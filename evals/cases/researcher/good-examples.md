# Researcher Output — Good Example

**Research request:** "What are mid-size companies (200-2,000 employees) currently citing as their top reasons for hiring an external executive coach in 2025-2026? I'm prepping talking points for outreach to HR/People leaders at this segment. Keep it to a quick scan — I need 3-5 bullets I can use in a cold email, not a report."

---

## Output

**Direct answer:** The three reasons that show up most consistently across 2025-2026 sources are (1) retaining newly promoted managers through a high-failure transition period, (2) building internal succession pipelines instead of relying on external hires, and (3) addressing manager burnout/turnover risk in a tight people-management labor market.

1. **First-90-days failure rate for new managers.** Multiple 2025 surveys put first-time manager failure or early turnover above 40%, and coaching is increasingly positioned as the retention fix rather than just a perk. Source: Gartner, "Reimagining the Manager Experience for a Hybrid World" (2025), https://www.gartner.com/en/human-resources/topics/managers — accessed and confirmed live.

2. **Succession planning pressure.** SHRM's 2025 State of the Workplace report found companies with 200-2,000 employees increasingly use coaching as a lower-cost alternative to external leadership hires for succession-critical roles. Source: SHRM, "State of the Workplace 2025," https://www.shrm.org/topics-tools/research/state-of-the-workplace-2025 — accessed and confirmed live.

3. **Manager burnout and turnover risk.** ICF's 2025 Global Coaching Study reports a measurable rise in employer-sponsored coaching specifically targeted at mid-level managers, with burnout prevention cited by employers as a top driver (vs. performance remediation, which was the top driver pre-2023). Source: International Coaching Federation, "2025 Global Coaching Study," https://coachingfederation.org/research/global-coaching-study — accessed and confirmed live.

4. **Budget framing shift.** Coaching is increasingly procured under "leadership development" or "retention" budget lines rather than "training," which matters for how you pitch — lead with retention ROI, not skill-building. Source: Training Industry, "2025 Leadership Development Benchmarking Report," https://trainingindustry.com/research/leadership-development-benchmarking-2025/ — accessed and confirmed live.

**So what for outreach:** Lead with the 90-day manager failure stat, not generic "unlock leadership potential" language — it's concrete, it's a budget-line match (retention, not training), and it's the angle HR/People leaders are already getting pressure on internally.

---

**Why this passes:**
- Directly answers the question asked (reasons companies hire coaches, for this audience, at this depth)
- Every claim has a named, credible, checkable source
- Appropriately concise for a quick-scan request — no padding
- Ends with a specific, usable "so what" tied to the stated downstream use (cold email talking points)
