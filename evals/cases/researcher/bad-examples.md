# Researcher Output — Bad Example

**Research request:** "What are mid-size companies (200-2,000 employees) currently citing as their top reasons for hiring an external executive coach in 2025-2026? I'm prepping talking points for outreach to HR/People leaders at this segment. Keep it to a quick scan — I need 3-5 bullets I can use in a cold email, not a report."

---

## Output

Executive coaching has become one of the fastest-growing categories in corporate learning and development. Organizations of all sizes are recognizing that leadership skills don't develop on their own, and coaching has emerged as a powerful tool for unlocking potential at every level.

Here's what's driving the growth of the coaching industry overall:

- **The rise of remote and hybrid work** has made leadership harder than ever, and companies are looking for ways to support managers who no longer have the benefit of in-person mentorship.
- **Generational shifts in the workforce** mean younger employees expect more personalized development than previous generations did, and coaching fits that expectation well.
- **The war for talent** continues to push companies to invest more in their people, and coaching is part of a broader trend toward employee experience investment.
- **AI and automation** are changing what leadership looks like, and coaches are helping executives navigate the transition.
- Coaching is also increasingly seen as a status symbol — having an executive coach signals that a leader is high-potential and worth investing in.

The coaching industry overall is projected to keep growing over the next several years, with the global market reportedly worth several billion dollars and increasing demand across industries. Many thought leaders in the HR space have written about this shift, and it's a topic that comes up frequently at conferences like SHRM and HR Tech.

Overall, this is a great time to be in the executive coaching business, and companies of all sizes are recognizing the value coaches bring.

---

**Why this fails:**
- **Answers a different question.** The request asked specifically why *mid-size companies (200-2,000 employees)* hire coaches *right now*, for use in cold outreach. The output instead summarizes why the coaching industry in general is growing — a broader, vaguer, and not-quite-the-same question.
- **No citations.** Claims like "projected to keep growing," "worth several billion dollars," and "thought leaders... have written about this" have no named source, no link, nothing checkable. It reads confidently but nothing in it can be verified.
- **No segment-specific or current-year grounding.** Nothing here is specific to 2025-2026 or to the 200-2,000 employee range — it would have read the same in 2019.
- **No actionable hook.** There's no bullet a salesperson could actually drop into a cold email; it's industry-trend filler rather than a usable angle.
- **Hyperbole instead of evidence.** Phrases like "great time to be in the executive coaching business" and "status symbol" are unsupported framing, not findings.
