# Avatar Profiles — Buyer Personas / ICPs

Six buyer types defined. Two are **not in active focus** (passive sales only). Four are active targets, with B2B being the primary growth direction.

---

## 1. C-Suite & Sr. Executive — PRIMARY B2B TARGET

**Focus:** Team Transformation, Talent Growth, Conflict & Crisis Management, Mediation

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Names | Annette, Michael |
| Titles | CEO, COO, C-suite; Director to VP-level with direct reports, functional + budget authority; some cases: Talent/HR lead |
| Age | 35–55 |
| Education | MA, BA, MBA |
| Org size | SMB 10–100M ARR, some high-growth startups; 10–1,000 employees |
| Social | LinkedIn, Twitter, YouTube, Instagram |
| Gains info via | Executive networks, LinkedIn, search, industry events/keynotes |

**Goals:** Fuel growth by transitioning team to new direction; develop leadership bench strength; achieve alignment, trust, and a team that works without extensive supervision.

**Biggest challenges:**
- Weak leadership bench; succession planning gaps
- Broken or weak leadership trust (C-level not trusting leaders to execute with autonomy)
- Ineffective communication; lack of cross-functional collaboration; silos
- Misalignment on new company direction; turnaround needed
- Leadership skills underdeveloped (traditional businesses, long-tenured employees)
- "Not-invented-here" syndrome; slow/flawed decision-making
- Interpersonal conflict; PIP employees; hidden conflict stalling key projects
- Trust gaps: risks aren't flagged, fear of retaliation, fixed mindset culture
- Recalibration after communication breakdown or critical event (loss of high performers, re-orgs)
- Need for collaboration and feedback culture to innovate and improve

**Common questions/objections:**
- How can I get my team to up-level in the direction we want to take next?
- How can I create a team that is more aligned and collaborative?
- How can we navigate the recent crisis/setback/org change and move forward?
- I've inherited this team and need to hit the ground running — we need to be a high-performing unit.
- Is it possible to turn around such a difficult team/scenario?
- Isn't the better option to let go of low-performers and hire replacements?

## 2. AI Practice Lab Buyer — B2B, L&D Focus

**Focus:** Scalable leadership development, AI-enabled L&D, between-session practice

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Names | Devin, Celisse, Kevin, Lisa, Anand, Ramona |
| Titles | VP HR, Dir of L&D, Head of Talent Development, Chief Talent Officer, CLO, Chief People Officer, Head of Digital Transformation, VP Innovation, Chief Transformation Officer |
| Age | 35–60 |
| Education | MA, BA, MBA |
| Org size | SME to Enterprise (250–100K+); sweet spot: 1K–50K employees with dedicated L&D function |
| Social | LinkedIn, industry conferences, HR Tech events |
| Gains info via | LinkedIn, TalentConnect, ATD, SHRM, peer networks, vendor demos, analyst reports, HR tech publications |

**Goals:** Scale leadership development without scaling headcount; provide between-session practice; build measurable communication skills; adopt AI with tangible ROI; modernize L&D.

**Mindset:** Innovation-forward, exploring AI-enabled solutions; not interested in more workshops alone; wants technology that extends existing programs; values privacy, security, and data.

**Biggest challenges:**
- Proving ROI of leadership development programs
- Scaling quality development beyond small cohorts
- Getting leaders to practice between sessions (not just attend)
- Privacy/security concerns with AI tools in enterprise
- Procurement timelines; internal skepticism about AI-powered development
- Budget justification for new technology categories

**Common questions/objections:**
- How does the AI handle sensitive leadership conversations?
- Where is the data stored?
- Can we build custom scenarios for our organization?
- What analytics do we get on usage and progress?
- How does this integrate with our existing L&D stack?
- Can we pilot this with a small group first?
- What's the ROI beyond engagement metrics?
- Is this a replacement for coaching or a complement?

**How AI Practice Lab fits the Zaradigm ecosystem:**
- HiPe TC workshop = ignition point (team reset)
- AI Practice Lab = practice layer (self-paced skill building)
- the/SHIFT = ongoing development (bi-weekly group coaching)
- **Procurement-friendly story:** "We're building leadership communication capability, not buying a one-time event."

**Differentiators for this buyer:**
- Built on proven coaching methodology (not generic AI prompts)
- Created by a certified business mediator and leadership communication expert
- Pairs with live team coaching and group programs (not standalone SaaS)
- Custom scenario development available for enterprise clients
- Practice on the leader's schedule, no facilitator needed

**Trigger events (buying windows):**
- Organization-wide AI adoption initiative
- Post-workshop follow-up need ("the workshop was great, but nothing changed")
- L&D budget review seeking scalable solutions
- New CHRO/CLO wanting to modernize the development stack
- M&A or restructuring creating urgent leadership communication needs

## 3. The Growing Leader — Active Individual Target (B2C transitioning to B2B-sponsored)

**Focus:** People leaders with direct reports; leader of leaders/function; not new to role

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Names | Kevin, Elodie, Michele |
| Titles | Manager, Director, Sr. Director, VP, GM |
| Age | 35–55 |
| Education | Varies |
| Org size | Enterprise, SME, rarely startup |
| Social | LinkedIn, Twitter, YouTube |
| Gains info via | Courses, podcasts, LinkedIn, conferences, books |

**Mindset:** Growth-focused, wants to learn and grow; open to feedback and different approaches; committed to applying themselves, showing up, practicing.

**Goals:** Achieving team/org goals; mobilizing teams/influence; confidence/overcoming impostor; balance/prioritizing; clarity (path forward); perception (brand, style).

**Biggest challenges:** Clarity on next steps; overcoming inner critic and doubt; discomfort with self-advocacy; delegating; skill development; carving out time for long-term career goals; brand/perception.

**Common questions:** What is next for me in my career? How can I overcome insecurities/become confident? How can I get promoted? How do I advocate for myself without bragging? How can I become seen for my work?

## 4. The Emerging Leader — Active Individual Target (New People Leader)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Names | Bryan, Erin |
| Titles | Manager – Director |
| Age | 30–50 |
| Education | MA, BA |
| Org size | Enterprise, SME |
| Social | LinkedIn, Twitter, YouTube |
| Gains info via | Courses, magazines, social, YouTube |

**Mindset:** Same as Growing Leader but more focus on "getting things right" — delegating well, working through others, prioritizing differently, elevating communication to leadership level, learning to navigate strategic thinking while balancing operational impact.

**Goals:** Getting promoted; achieving team/org goals; balance/prioritizing; gaining confidence; clarity; fulfillment; perception/influence.

**Biggest challenges:** Communicating with executives; delegating effectively; advocacy (self and team); boosting employee morale; problem solving and decision making; skill development; managing conflict; improving team performance; personal productivity; political savvy; managing up and down; high-stakes conversations (PIP, board meeting); strategic skills.
