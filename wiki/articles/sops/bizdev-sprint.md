**Subject:** "Your employer probably covers this"
**Trigger:** Warm leads/clickers who haven't converted — removes price barrier by surfacing employer L&D budget
**Template stored in HubSpot:** "the/SHIFT employer covers this" (ID 124280902)

**Email structure:**
1. Opener — name + one line on why you're reaching out (or just "I wanted to make the decision easier for you to join the/SHIFT.")
2. Short the/SHIFT description: "a leadership group meeting twice a month to practice key skills together in a safe space..."
3. *Most employers will cover the cost if you ask them.* Many don't because they don't know they could, or aren't sure how to ask. Template offer + call offer.

## Email Voice Correction Log (from actual sent emails, May–June 2026)

Learned from comparing Claude drafts vs. what Corinna actually sent:

| Pattern | Claude draft | Corinna's correction |
|---------|-------------|---------------------|
| Coaching alumni opener | "When we wrapped up our Torch coaching..." | Ask about their life first (baby, family, job) before any business |
| Personal grounding | No reference to personal experience | Added "I can relate, I've lived it." (Jaime email) |
| CTA for warm contacts | Stripe link included | Removed payment link; "Let me know and I'll send more details" |
| Dialogue vs. close | "You know I expect an invitation" | "What's planned this summer?" — opens dialogue |
| Subject lines | "the/SHIFT — June 16" (em-dash) | "the/SHIFT starts June 16" (no em-dash) |
| Secondary CTA | "or reply if you want to talk" | "or reply with questions" (more specific) |
