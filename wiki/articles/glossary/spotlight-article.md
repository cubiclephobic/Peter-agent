# Spotlight Article

## Definition

A CMAG article that addresses a topic the ICP (coaches building businesses) genuinely cares about or is curious about, while subtly — not overtly — referencing one partner organization as the solution to the challenges discussed.

## What makes it work

- Reads like an explainer or educational piece, not an advertisement
- The partner is referenced as the natural solution, not pushed
- The topic must have genuine value to the reader independent of the partner mention
- One partner only — exclusivity is part of the value proposition to the partner

## What makes it fail

- Reads promotional — if the reader notices it's an ad, it's broken
- Multiple partner mentions — dilutes the exclusivity and the editorial credibility
- Topic chosen for the partner's benefit, not the reader's interest

## Example

The RankMath article explaining how to make blog posts discoverable by GPTs (beyond traditional SEO). Topic: genuinely useful to coaches with content. Partner: RankMath referenced as the tool that handles it. Reception: read as an explainer, not an ad.
