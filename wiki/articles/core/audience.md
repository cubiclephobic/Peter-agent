# Audience — Who I Create For and Serve

## Primary Audiences (Active, Revenue-Generating)

### 1. Organizational Buyers — B2B (Top Priority)

**Who:** C-suite, HR/L&D leaders, business owners who sponsor coaching or consulting for their teams — not for themselves personally.

**Titles:** CEO, COO, VP HR, Director of L&D, Head of Talent Development, Chief People Officer, Chief Transformation Officer, Head of Digital Transformation

**Org size:** SMB ($10M–$100M ARR, 10–1,000 employees) to mid-size enterprise (1K–50K employees with dedicated L&D function)

**What they're buying:** Team performance, leadership communication, conflict resolution, or AI-enabled practice at scale. They pay for outcomes, not sessions.

**Why they hire Zaradigm:** The mediation background is the differentiator — no competitor brings conflict resolution capability to leadership communication coaching. "One conflict away from formal mediation" is a real buying moment.

**Trigger events:**
- Post-workshop follow-up need ("the workshop was great, but nothing changed")
- M&A or restructuring creating urgent leadership communication needs
- New CHRO/CLO wanting to modernize L&D stack
- Organization-wide AI adoption initiative
- L&D budget review seeking scalable solutions

---

### 2. Emerging and Mid-Level Leaders — B2B-Sponsored (the/SHIFT, HiPe TC)

**Who:** People leaders (Manager through VP) inside organizations whose employer sponsors their development.

**What they need:** Practice under pressure, not more theory. Real high-stakes conversations: conflict, managing up, performance, change.

**Persona names in use:** Kevin, Elodie, Michele (growing leaders); Bryan, Erin (emerging/new people leaders)

**Pain pattern:** Capable individually, but their communication and visibility lag behind their contribution. They've attended trainings that didn't change anything because there was no practice.

---

### 3. Independent Coaching Clients — B2C (Winding Down; Existing Only)

**Who:** Individual executives and leaders buying coaching for themselves. No longer actively prospecting.

**Platform:** Torch (structured, contained 45-min sessions), Zaradigm direct (higher-touch, async between sessions, AI Practice Lab access)

**Status:** Still delivering to existing clients; no new B2C acquisition.

---

### 4. Coaches — CMAG Audience (Media Play)

**Who:** Life, executive, business, career, and niche coaches (not professional sports coaches) building sustainable coaching businesses.

**Stage:** Aspiring and early-stage through growing solo practices and small coaching teams.

**What they need:** More clients, better systems, higher profitability, tool guidance without jargon.

**Key insight:** Strong coaches, often less confident in business fundamentals. Write so a smart coach with no formal business background can follow along — without being condescending.

**Brand:** Coachilly Mag (CMAG) — separate from Zaradigm, never blend.

## Content Audience vs. Paying Audience

**Content audience (LinkedIn, the/SHIFT newsletter, Substack):** Emerging and established leaders in organizations — broader than the direct buyer. Content builds trust and visibility with people who influence buying decisions.

**Paying audience (direct revenue):** Organizational buyers (B2B), Torch/Zaradigm coaching clients (B2C winding down), CMAG readers (passive/affiliate, future).
