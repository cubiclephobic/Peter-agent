# Coachilly Mag — Master Brand Reference

## About the Brand

COACHILLY MAG is the go-to digital magazine for coaches who want to build and grow sustainable, profitable, future-proof coaching businesses. Owned by Zaradigm LLC. Separate brand, separate ICP, separate design system from Zaradigm — never blend.

**Use "COACHILLY MAG"** when referring to the magazine. **Use "Coachilly"** for the broader brand, community, or services.
**Status:** Active content brand with passive income potential at scale. B2C coaching (Coachilly coaching) is winding down. CMAG itself is the media play.
**Newsletter name:** Currently "the dispatch" — rename is a future decision.

---

## Mission & Vision

**Mission:** Help coaches build thriving, profitable coaching businesses and stay current on business, tech, and industry trends.

**Vision:** A world where coaches have the tools, knowledge, and confidence to turn their expertise into a sustainable business and meaningful impact.

---

## ICP

**Who:** Life, executive, business, career, and niche coaches (not professional sports coaches).
**Stage:** Aspiring and early-stage through growing solo practices and small coaching teams.
**Needs:** More clients, better systems, higher profitability, tool guidance without jargon or overwhelm.

**Key insight:** Readers are strong coaches, often less confident in business fundamentals (marketing, sales, technology, systems, financial management). Write so a smart coach with no formal business background can follow along — without being condescending.

## Brand Voice

### Core Principles

| Principle | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| Clear, not clever | Plain language first |
| Teacher, not guru | Share knowledge, don't preach |
| Ambitious but grounded | Real goals, realistic paths |
| Friendly challenger | Challenge bad assumptions, not the reader |

### Signature Voice Characteristics

Same as Zaradigm's signature voice — pattern-spotting, impatient with fluff, grounded in real moments, solution-focused, clean analogies, slightly irreverent, humble about complexity. The difference is the subject matter: coaching business, not corporate leadership communication.

### Formatting Rules

- Periods over em-dashes — avoid em-dashes entirely (AI-stigma)
- Bold sparingly — key insights only
- Short paragraphs (3–5 sentences max)
- No rhetorical questions — replace with direct statements
- No: "delve", "tapestry", "landscape", "underscore", "crucial", "pivotal", "showcase", "testament"
- No: "In today's ever-evolving…", "whether you're a seasoned X or just beginning your journey"

### Guardrail: Conviction Without Arrogance

❌ "Coaches who don't ask for help are failing themselves and their organizations."
✅ "Many coaches believe asking for help signals weakness. In reality, it signals commitment to growth — and it's the difference between stalling and advancing."

### Jargon Rules

Avoid unnecessary jargon. When business terms must be used (funnel, conversion, CAC), define briefly in plain language on first use.

## Content Types

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Industry Update / Trend Piece** | What's changing and why it matters to coaches. Must connect trends to practical implications for running a coaching business. Focus: how to respond in next 3–12 months. |
| **Comparison Article** | Helps coaches choose between tools, platforms, or approaches. Structure reduces decision fatigue: who it's for, core features, pricing, key trade-offs. Neutral even with affiliate relationships. |
| **Spotlight Article** | Deep dive into one tool/service solving a meaningful problem. Lead with the problem, then show the solution practically. Include realistic use cases and integration guidance. Disclose commercial relationships. |
| **Opinion / Perspective** | Challenges assumptions or hype. Bold but fair, grounded in experience or research. Must land on practical implications — not just critique. |
| **How-To / Step-by-Step** | Translates complex topics into clear processes. Break into logical stages, specify what to do, in what order, and why. Include sample scripts or templates where possible. |
| **Substack Derivative** | Shortest version of any longform piece — one idea, one takeaway |
| **Social Snippet** | 1–2 sentence quote-style unit extracted from longform; used in graphics or LinkedIn posts |

See `articles/glossary/spotlight-article.md` for the full Spotlight Article definition.

### Content Standards (All Types)

- Every sentence serves a clear purpose — substance over length
- No filler language, generic intros, or empty promises
- No clichés or exaggerated income claims
- Results framed as realistic possibilities requiring effort, not guarantees
- Acknowledge nuance and limitations so readers make informed decisions
- SEO matters but never overrides clarity or reader value — readability takes priority
- Verify pricing, features, and claims at time of writing
- Write with repurposing in mind: clear subheadings, distinct key points, concise section summaries
- Reflect diversity of coaching industry across examples, scenarios, and imagery over time

## Visual Identity

### Colors

| Role | Name | Hex |
|------|------|-----|
| Primary | Pop Red | `#ff260f` |
| Primary | Black | `#000000` |
| Secondary | Lichtenstein Red | `#f7200c` |

| Secondary | Popart Tan | `#f6cea8` |
| Secondary | Light Gray | `#d6d6d6` |

**Note:** Use red mainly for accents, CTAs, and emphasis — avoid full red backgrounds for long text.

### Typography

| Use | Font | Notes |
|-----|------|-------|
| Logo name + tagline | Agrandir | Logo only — never elsewhere |
| All headlines | Nunito Extra Bold / Bold | |
| All body text | Nunito Regular | -2 letter spacing, 1.5 line height |
| Tight spaces | Kollektif | Only when space is constrained |

**Type scale:** Title 64pt → Heading 32pt → Subtitle 28pt → Section 18pt → Subheading 16pt → Body 12pt → Quote 16pt → Caption 9pt

### Visual Motif

- Pop-art inspired with bold color contrasts
- **NO pop-art dots** — dots are Zaradigm's motif, not CMAG's
- Bold flat shapes, strong outlines in moderation; playful but professional
- Light neutral backgrounds for most social graphics and infographics

### Photography

- Coaches at laptops, on calls, in workshops; diverse demographics
- Avoid overly corporate boardroom imagery unless specifically relevant
- Emotionally honest but composed — focus, insight, lightness
- Not staged or dramatic

### Icons

- Flat, bold lines, one primary brand color per icon
- No hairline, 3D, skeuomorphic, or gradient icons
- No decorative use — icons clarify meaning only
