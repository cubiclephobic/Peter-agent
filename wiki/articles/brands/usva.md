# US Virtual Academy (USVA) — Client Brand Reference

## About the Brand

US Virtual Academy is an independent, fully accredited American private high school operating entirely online. Serves students in all 50 states and internationally: athletes, neurodivergent learners, students who struggled in traditional schools, military families, international students, and anyone for whom the standard school system hasn't worked.

**Tagline:** Empowering learners. Everywhere.
**Three brand words:** Welcoming (Navy) · Credible (Red) · Empowering (Gold)
**Brand statement:** Warmth, credibility, and empowerment in every color choice, word, and image.

**Relationship to Corinna/Zaradigm:** USVA is a client. Zaradigm created this brand kit in 2026. Content and assets produced for USVA must follow this brand, not Zaradigm's.

---

## Mission & Vision

**Mission:** Provide a warm, rigorous, and fully accredited American high school education to any student, anywhere — meeting them where they are and building a lasting relationship with every learner.

**Vision:** A world where no student is left behind because of geography, circumstance, or learning difference.

---

## Target Audience

USVA serves two audiences simultaneously. Voice stays consistent — emphasis shifts by context.

**Parents** (primary decision-makers)
- Need to trust the diploma is legitimate, their student will be supported, they won't have to become a teacher
- Tone: Reassuring, protective, partnership-focused
- Key words: "partner," "support," "guidance," "certified," "accredited," "recognized"
- Core message: You are not alone. We support your student and you.

**Students** (end users, ages 14–22; some engage independently with no parent)
- Want freedom, flexibility, to be seen and respected, real relationships
- Tone: Empowering, possibility-focused, peer-level respect
- Key words: "your pace," "your choice," "your future," "connect," "community," "real"
- Core message: This is your education. You're in control. You're not alone.

**Student types served:**
- Athletes & performers
- Neurodivergent & autistic students
- At-risk students
- International students
- Military families

## Brand Voice

**Five words:** Warm · Confident · Clear · Student-Centric · Empowering

| Principle | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Warm | Speak like a person, not a system. Meet people where they are. | "We know handing over your student's education takes trust. We don't take that lightly." |
| Confident | Know what we do, say it directly. No hedging. | "USVA graduates earn a fully accredited US high school diploma. Full stop." |
| Clear | No jargon. If it takes two reads, rewrite it. | "You be the parent. We'll handle the academics." |
| Student-Centric | Every message filtered through: how does this serve the student? | "We didn't design USVA for the average student. We designed it for yours." |
| Empowering | Give people agency. Don't rescue — equip. | "Your student leads their education here. We make sure they have everything they need." |

**NOT:** Corporate or stiff · Condescending · Overly casual · Vague or buzzword-heavy

### Messaging Pillars

1. **Legitimacy** — "Fully accredited US high school diploma." "Recognized by universities globally." "Certified US teachers."
2. **Flexibility** — "Learn from anywhere, any time." "Your pace. Your schedule."
3. **Support** — "You be the parent. We handle the academics." "Certified teachers, not tutors or bots."
4. **Community** — "Virtual, not alone." "Connected cohorts, not isolated learners."
5. **Opportunity** — "Gateway to US universities and beyond." "Education without borders."

### AI Content Standards

Same as Zaradigm/CMAG: avoid "delve," "tapestry," "landscape," "underscore," "crucial," "pivotal," "showcase," "testament." No "in today's ever-evolving…" No generic lists. All AI content must be reviewed and shaped to reflect USVA voice before publishing.

## Visual Identity

### Colors

| Role | Name | Hex | Use |
|------|------|-----|-----|
| Primary | American Flag Navy | `#0A3161` | Primary backgrounds, headers, nav, buttons, body text on light backgrounds |
| Primary | American Flag Red | `#BF0A30` | Accents, patriotic identity moments, secondary CTAs — never dominant |
| Accent | Golden Bronze | `#B3942E` | Primary CTA button, logo tassel, hover states, premium accents — use sparingly |
| Neutral | Light Gray | `#E8E8E8` | Dividers, card backgrounds, section breaks |
| Neutral | White | `#FFFFFF` | Primary page backgrounds, text on dark surfaces |
| Neutral | Text Charcoal | `#333333` | Body text on white or light gray |

**Combinations that work:** Navy bg + white text + gold CTA | White bg + navy text + red accent | Light gray bg + navy text + gold icons | Navy + white + red together (trust bar)
**Combinations to avoid:** Red on gold | Gold text on white for body copy | Red as full section background

### Typography

**Primary font:** Poppins (Google Fonts — free)

| Weight | Use |
|--------|-----|
| Bold 700 | Headlines, hero text, section titles |
| SemiBold 600 | Sub-headers, H3, featured labels |
| Medium 500 | Navigation, button text, captions, labels |
| Regular 400 | Body copy, FAQ answers, descriptions |

**Type scale:** Title 42pt → Subtitle 36pt → Heading 32pt → Subheading 24pt → Section 20pt → Body 12–14pt → Quote 16pt → Caption 9–12pt

### Logo Rules

- Primary mark: all formal communications, website header, printed materials, email signatures
- Icon-only: social media profiles, favicons
- Clear space: equal to height of "U" on all sides
- Use only on navy, white, or light gray backgrounds
- Never: stretch, rotate, skew, distort, change colors, add effects, use full logo below 120px wide

### Photography

- Students in non-traditional learning spaces (kitchen table, porch, living room)
- Athletes with laptop nearby — two worlds coexisting
- Calm, focused learner with natural light; no chaos
- International students with different settings visible
- Parents nearby but not hovering — peaceful coexistence
- Students on video call — community feel
- Avoid: traditional classrooms, forced stock smiles, all-white/single-demographic casts, overly styled staging, phones for social media, anything implying struggle

**Real beats perfect** — slightly imperfect photo of a real-looking student outperforms flawless stock.

**Image treatment:** Natural color preferred, warm tones welcomed. Full-width backgrounds: navy overlay 35–45% opacity with white text over.

### Icons

- Style: Minimalist line icons — Phosphor Icons library (PNGs, light weight)
- Small/UI: 20–24px | Standard: 32–48px | Large: 64px
- On white/light gray: Navy or Gold | On navy: White or Gold
- Never use Red for icons alone

### Cards & UI Elements

- Cards: white background, 12px border radius, soft shadow (0px 2px 12px rgba(0,0,0,0.08))
- Dividers: 1px solid Light Gray, horizontal only
- Accent shapes: subtle geometric elements in Gold (thin lines, small circles)
- No gradients, no patterns, no drop shadows on icons
