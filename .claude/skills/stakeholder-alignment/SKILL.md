# stakeholder-alignment

Map who needs to be aligned to move a strategy forward, identify where gaps exist, and design the influence approach for each stakeholder. First skill in the Communicate stage. Especially relevant when Corinna is leading change inside a client organization.

Run as: `/stakeholder-alignment [strategy or change initiative]`

---

## Behavior

### 1. Identify the stakeholders

List everyone whose support, neutrality, or non-resistance is required for the strategy to succeed. Include:
- **Decision-makers:** who can approve, fund, or block the initiative?
- **Influencers:** who shapes the decision-makers' views, even without formal authority?
- **Implementers:** who has to actually do something differently for this to work?
- **Potential blockers:** who has something to lose, or whose identity or interests conflict with the change?

For client engagements, cross-reference any people wiki articles (`wiki/articles/people/`) for relationship context before mapping. Never reduce a person to a stakeholder role in any client-facing output.

### 2. Assess alignment and power

For each stakeholder: where are they on two axes?
- **Power:** high (can stop or accelerate the initiative) vs. low.
- **Current alignment:** strong supporter, neutral/uninformed, skeptic, active blocker.

The quadrant that matters most: **high power + not yet aligned** — these are the alignment priorities.

### 3. Identify the alignment gaps

What specifically is each high-priority stakeholder missing, worried about, or resistant to? Don't assume resistance is irrational — name the legitimate concern. Common alignment gaps:
- Insufficient information (they don't understand what's changing or why).
- Insufficient evidence (they don't believe the strategy will work).
- Perceived loss (they see a cost to themselves that hasn't been addressed).
- Process concern (they support the goal but object to the path).

### 4. Design the influence approach

For each high-priority stakeholder, name:
- **The conversation that needs to happen:** one-on-one, group setting, formal presentation, or informal update?
- **Who should have it:** Corinna, or a more credible voice for this specific person?
- **The message:** what does this person need to hear to move from current alignment to sufficient alignment? Not the same message for everyone.
- **The sequence:** who needs to be aligned before others? Getting the right person aligned early creates momentum; getting the wrong sequence creates resistance.

### 5. Output

**Format (scannable in under five minutes):**

- **Stakeholder map:** power × alignment for each person — a short table is fine.
- **Alignment priorities:** the 2–3 stakeholders where misalignment most threatens the initiative.
- **Gap diagnosis:** what specifically each priority stakeholder needs.
- **Influence plan:** for each priority — conversation type, messenger, message, sequence.
- **So what:** handoff to `narrative-builder` (craft the story for the key conversations) or `decision-memo` (produce the formal recommendation for a high-power decision-maker).

### 6. Guardrails

- This skill maps and plans the alignment work. It does not draft the conversations or presentations — that's `narrative-builder` or `decision-memo`.
- Never characterize a person as a "blocker" in any client-facing output. Stakeholder mapping is an internal planning tool only.
- Influence is not manipulation. The goal is genuine alignment through the right information and honest conversation — not circumventing legitimate concerns.
